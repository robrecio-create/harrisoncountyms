Most people come to Harrison County for the casino strip or the beaches. Both are fine. But the county has a lot more if you know where to look.

## Ship Island (Worth Repeating)

Yes, it's in every guide. Yes, it's still the most underrated thing on the Coast. The ferry from Gulfport takes thirty minutes and puts you on an island that looks more like the Florida Panhandle than Mississippi. Go on a weekday in late spring or early fall — the summer crowds and the winter cold are both avoidable.

## Beauvoir Grounds

The Beauvoir estate — Jefferson Davis's last home — gets some visitors, but most people drive past it on Highway 90. The grounds themselves are open and worth a slow walk. Live oaks, Gulf views, and a sense of how the coast looked before development.

## Pass Christian Small Boat Harbor

Most tourists go to the Pass Christian beach and leave. The small boat harbor a block away is where the locals actually hang out. Pelicans, sailboats, fishermen cleaning their catch. Good for an hour of doing nothing.

## The Saucier Area

Saucier is in northern Harrison County, away from the coast entirely. Piney woods, small farms, a slower pace. DeSoto National Forest is nearby. If you've had enough Gulf Coast sun, this is where you go.

## Long Beach Harbor

Long Beach has its own small harbor area that doesn't get much attention compared to Gulfport and Biloxi. Quieter, more local, and a good sunset spot.

## The Back Bay of Biloxi

The Back Bay area north of the casino strip has some of the city's oldest neighborhoods and best local restaurants. The tourist traffic doesn't make it back here. That's your advantage.

## Fishing Piers

Multiple public fishing piers stretch into the Gulf along Highway 90. No boat required. Locals fish from them regularly. You can just walk out and watch if you're not angling.
