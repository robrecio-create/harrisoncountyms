Biloxi has the most diverse restaurant scene on the Mississippi Gulf Coast. The casinos bring in bigger name restaurants, but the best local spots are off the casino strip.

## Beyond the Buffet

The casino buffets are famous for a reason — value, variety, seafood. But if you're eating every meal at a casino you're missing what Biloxi actually has to offer.

The old downtown area near the Biloxi Lighthouse and the Back Bay have some of the city's best independent restaurants. Smaller, locally owned, and cooking things the casinos don't bother with.

## Seafood

Biloxi has deep roots in the seafood industry — shrimping and oyster harvesting built this town. Several spots still source locally.

**Back Bay** restaurants tend to be more casual and focused on simple preparations: boiled, fried, or grilled with local spices. No frills, good fish.

## Vietnamese Food

Biloxi has a significant Vietnamese community, and their restaurants are excellent and underrated. Pho, banh mi, and fresh rolls at prices that make you wonder why you'd eat anywhere else for lunch.

## The Old Point Area

The neighborhood around the old Point Cadet area has some of the city's oldest restaurants. Family operations that have been around for decades, serving the same dishes with minimal pretense.

## Tips

- **Avoid peak casino weekends** if you want quick service at popular spots
- **Lunch deals** are everywhere — the nicer places often do half-price specials
- **Ask locals** — anyone who's lived here knows the three or four places that don't disappoint
