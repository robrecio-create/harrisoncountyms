Biloxi's dining scene spans centuries-old fine dining, working-waterfront seafood houses, and some of the best casino restaurants on the Gulf Coast. Whether you want white-tablecloth elegance or a paper-plate po'boy by the water, Biloxi delivers.

## Iconic Local Restaurants

**Mary Mahoney's Old French House** is the crown jewel of Biloxi dining. Operating since 1964 inside an 1737 French colonial building on Rue Magnolia, it serves refined Gulf seafood and steaks in one of the most atmospheric settings in Mississippi. The courtyard dining beneath the ancient live oak is unforgettable. Reservations are strongly recommended, especially on weekends.

**Vestige** has earned national attention for its creative tasting menus built around seasonal Southern ingredients. Chef Alex Perry's intimate restaurant focuses on locally sourced produce and Gulf seafood prepared with modern technique. It is one of the most acclaimed fine-dining destinations in the entire state.

**Half Shell Oyster House** is a Gulf Coast institution with a Biloxi location that stays busy for good reason. Chargrilled oysters, po'boys, and platters of fried Gulf shrimp are consistently excellent. The atmosphere is casual enough for families but polished enough for date night.

## Casual Favorites

**The Fillin' Station** is a laid-back neighborhood spot where locals gather for burgers, seafood baskets, and cold beer. **Shaggy's Biloxi Beach** sits right on the sand with open-air seating, frozen drinks, and fried seafood platters that match the relaxed Gulf Coast vibe perfectly. **White Pilgrim** offers a more eclectic menu with craft cocktails in a cozy setting that draws a loyal following.

## Back Bay Seafood

Some of Biloxi's most authentic seafood is found along the Back Bay, where the city's shrimping and oystering heritage runs deep. **McElroy's Harbor House** overlooks the Biloxi Small Craft Harbor and serves straightforward Gulf seafood — boiled shrimp, fried catfish, and gumbo — in a waterfront setting that feels distinctly local. The view of the working shrimp boats adds character you will not find at a casino.

## Casino Dining

Biloxi's casino resorts bring serious culinary talent to the Coast. **BR Prime** at Beau Rivage is a top-tier steakhouse with an extensive wine list and Gulf views from the upper floors. **Hard Rock Hotel and Casino** offers several dining options ranging from casual to upscale. The casino restaurants are especially convenient for weekend visitors who want quality dining without venturing far from their hotel.

## Tips for Dining in Biloxi

- Weekday lunches at the nicer spots often feature special pricing and shorter waits
- The Vietnamese restaurants near Point Cadet are excellent and affordable — do not overlook them
- Peak casino weekends mean longer waits at popular spots across the city, not just on the resort strip
