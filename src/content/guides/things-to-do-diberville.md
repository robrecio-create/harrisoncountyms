D'Iberville sits just north of Biloxi, separated from the beachfront by the Back Bay of Biloxi. It is the fastest-growing city in Harrison County and has quietly built up a mix of entertainment, shopping, and dining options that make it a practical and fun base for exploring the Gulf Coast.

## Scarlet Pearl Casino Resort

The **Scarlet Pearl Casino Resort** is D'Iberville's flagship entertainment destination. Opened in 2015, it brought a full-scale casino, hotel, and dining complex to the city. The gaming floor covers over 50,000 square feet with slots, table games, and a poker room. Beyond gambling, Scarlet Pearl stands out for its restaurants. **Chophouse** serves quality steaks in an upscale setting, while several casual options cover everything from Southern comfort food to sushi. The resort also hosts live entertainment and events throughout the year, giving D'Iberville nightlife that holds its own against the beachfront casinos in Biloxi.

## The Promenade

**The Promenade** is an open-air shopping and entertainment center along Promenade Parkway that has become the retail heart of Harrison County. National retailers, local boutiques, restaurants, and a movie theater are all arranged around pedestrian-friendly walkways. It is the kind of place where you can spend a full afternoon browsing stores, grabbing lunch, and catching a movie. During the holiday season, the Promenade adds lights and special events that draw families from across the county.

## Big Play Entertainment Center

For families with kids or groups looking for something active, **Big Play Entertainment Center** is a large indoor recreation facility with bowling lanes, laser tag, an arcade, bumper cars, and a ropes course. It is a reliable rainy-day option and a popular spot for birthday parties and group outings. The facility is well maintained and spacious even on busy weekends.

## Lava Links Golf

**Lava Links Golf** offers miniature golf with a volcanic theme that appeals to families and anyone looking for a casual outdoor activity. The courses are well kept and provide a fun break from more structured attractions. It is an easy stop to pair with dinner or shopping at the nearby Promenade.

## Biloxi Bay Fishing

D'Iberville's location on the Back Bay of Biloxi gives it access to some of the best inshore fishing in Harrison County. The bay produces speckled trout, redfish, flounder, and sheepshead depending on the season. Several boat launches and fishing piers along the bayfront provide access for anglers with their own gear. Charter captains from nearby marinas offer guided trips, and kayak fishing has grown popular in the shallow marsh flats.

## Central Location

One of D'Iberville's biggest advantages is geography. Biloxi's beaches and casinos are five minutes south across the bay. Gulfport is fifteen minutes west on I-10. Hotel rates and restaurant prices tend to be lower than the beachfront, making D'Iberville a smart home base for visitors who want to explore the whole coast without paying resort premiums.
