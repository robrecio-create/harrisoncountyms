D'Iberville sits just north of Biloxi, separated from the coast by the Bay of Biloxi. It's less of a destination and more of a place people live — but it's grown fast and has more going on than most people realize.

## Shopping

D'Iberville has become Harrison County's retail hub. The **Promenade** is an open-air shopping center with national retailers, restaurants, and a movie theater. If you need to shop, this is where most of the county goes.

The area around Highway 49 has big-box stores, chain restaurants, and all the usual suburban commercial development. Not charming, but convenient.

## Eat

Several solid local restaurants have opened in D'Iberville as the population has grown. The dining scene skews toward casual — the kind of place where you know the owner and the portions are generous.

Look for local seafood spots that cater to the residential crowd rather than tourists. They're often better value than their beachside counterparts.

## Tchoutacabouffa River

The Tchoutacabouffa River flows near D'Iberville and offers fishing and kayaking for those who know to look for it. It's not well-publicized, but locals use it regularly. The Tchoutacabouffa River Park on the Biloxi side has put-in access.

## Location Advantage

D'Iberville is well-positioned: Biloxi and its beaches are five minutes south, Gulfport is fifteen minutes west, and I-10 connects you quickly to the rest of the Gulf Coast. It's a practical base for exploring without casino-strip prices.
