Gulfport is Harrison County's largest city and its dining scene reflects that. You'll find everything from waterfront seafood shacks to proper sit-down restaurants with Gulf views.

## Seafood

You're on the Gulf Coast — start with the seafood. Local spots pull in fresh shrimp, crab, and redfish regularly. Look for places that list their suppliers; the ones that brag about local boats are usually the ones worth your time.

Boiled shrimp, grilled redfish, and fried oysters are the Gulf Coast standards. Most good seafood places around Gulfport will do all three competently.

## Downtown Gulfport

The 25th Avenue area has seen some restaurant growth in recent years. Small, independently owned spots with rotating menus. If a place has been there more than five years, it's probably doing something right.

**Lunch** is often the better value meal at nicer spots — same kitchen, lower prices, shorter waits.

## Casual

There's no shortage of casual spots: po'boy shops, burger joints, and the inevitable chain restaurants along Highway 90. The locals know which independents are worth the detour.

## The Waterfront

Gulfport's waterfront along Highway 90 has several restaurants with Gulf views. The view adds something, but don't let it be the only reason you choose a place. The best waterfront spots also happen to have solid food.

## What to Order

- **Gulf shrimp** — boiled, fried, or in étouffée
- **Redfish on the half shell** — a Gulf Coast specialty
- **Crab claws** — seasonal, worth it when available
- **Beignets** — not New Orleans, but you'll find them nearby

## Getting Around

Gulfport is spread out. Most restaurants require a car. Highway 90 runs the length of the coast and has the highest concentration of options, but the better independent spots are often a few blocks inland.
