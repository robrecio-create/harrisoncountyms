Pass Christian (locals say "Pass Chris-tee-ANN") is the kind of town that makes people want to move to the Gulf Coast. It's small, historic, walkable, and hasn't been overrun by development.

## The Historic District

Pass Christian has one of the best collections of antebellum architecture on the Mississippi Gulf Coast. The old homes along the beach road are worth a slow drive or a walk. Many date to the mid-1800s; some survived Katrina with significant damage and were rebuilt faithfully.

**The Harbor** is a working small-boat harbor — sailboats, fishing boats, a small marina. The surrounding park area is pleasant for an afternoon.

## Beach

Pass Christian's beach is quieter than Biloxi or Gulfport. That's the point. Fewer crowds, same Gulf water, and a much more relaxed vibe. The beach runs right along the main highway through town.

## Dining

Small-town dining, but quality. A handful of restaurants have been in Pass Christian for years, serving the kind of food that keeps locals loyal. Seafood is always on the menu. Ask around for who's doing well lately — the scene shifts.

The local bakeries and coffee shops tend to be excellent and unhurried.

## Shopping

A handful of antique shops, boutiques, and art galleries along the main street. Not a shopping destination, but worth a browse if you're already there.

## Pass Christian Yacht Club

One of the oldest yacht clubs in Mississippi. The sailing scene here is real — regattas happen in season. If you sail or want to watch, it's worth a visit.

## Getting There

Pass Christian is about fifteen miles west of Gulfport on Highway 90. The drive along the coast is scenic. Give yourself an extra half-hour to just drive slowly and look at the houses.
