Long Beach is the quieter, more intimate neighbor between Gulfport and Pass Christian, and its restaurant scene reflects that small-town coastal charm. There are no casino buffets or chain-heavy strips here — just a handful of locally owned spots where the food is personal and the pace is unhurried.

## Harbor Area Dining

**Steve's Marina Restaurant** sits near the Long Beach Harbor and has been a fixture of the community for years. Known for fresh Gulf seafood served in generous portions, it is the kind of place where the shrimp came off a boat that morning and the staff knows most of the customers by name. Fried platters, boiled seafood, and po'boys are the main draws. The harbor setting gives it a working-waterfront atmosphere that feels genuine because it is.

## Downtown and Main Street

**The Blind Tiger** is one of Long Beach's most popular restaurants, offering a menu that goes beyond typical coastal fare. Steaks, pasta, and creative seafood dishes are served in a warm, inviting space that works equally well for a casual dinner or a special occasion. The bar program is solid, and the staff is attentive without being overbearing. It has earned a loyal following from across Harrison County, not just Long Beach residents.

**200 North Beach** brings an upscale-casual approach to Gulf Coast dining, with a menu that emphasizes fresh, seasonal ingredients and thoughtful preparation. Located near the heart of town, it offers a refined experience that still feels relaxed and approachable — very much in keeping with Long Beach's overall character.

## Coffee and Daytime Spots

**Long Beach Coffee Company** is a favorite morning stop for locals. Good coffee, pastries, and a welcoming atmosphere make it a natural gathering place. It is the kind of neighborhood coffee shop that anchors a community — a place to run into friends, catch up, or settle in with a laptop for a while.

## What Makes Long Beach Dining Different

Long Beach leans into what it does best: small, quality-focused restaurants where the owners are usually on-site and the menus reflect what is fresh and local. You will not find a dozen options on every block, but the places that are here tend to be genuinely good.

Several of the best restaurants are within a short distance of each other near the harbor and Jeff Davis Avenue, making it easy to park once and explore on foot.

## Tips

- Weeknight dining is relaxed and wait times are minimal
- Weekend evenings at the more popular spots can fill up — call ahead if your group is larger than four
- Long Beach is an easy drive from Gulfport or Pass Christian, making it a great midpoint dinner destination
